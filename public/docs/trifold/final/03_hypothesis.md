# Hypothesis

Adolescents with higher diet quality scores — characterized by higher fiber and omega-3 intake proxies and lower added sugar and saturated fat intake — are expected to show higher cognitive test performance, particularly in language and logical reasoning tasks.

This trend is biologically plausible because diet can influence gut microbial fermentation products, including short-chain fatty acids, which are associated with inflammation regulation and neurotransmitter-related signaling pathways. (This project tests associations in the pilot data and explores mechanistic hypotheses using modeled proxy layers.)
