# Hypothesis

Adolescents with higher diet quality scores — characterized by higher fiber and omega-3 intake proxies and lower added sugar and saturated fat intake — will demonstrate higher cognitive test performance, particularly in language and logical reasoning tasks.

This relationship is biologically plausible because diet can influence gut microbial fermentation products, including short-chain fatty acids, which are associated with inflammation regulation and neurotransmitter-related signaling pathways.
